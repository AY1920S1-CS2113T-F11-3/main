Dear KEVIIans

 

Following OSHE’s email on haze management, may I please refer you to item 2(f), pages 4 to 5 of the NUS Safety SOP (standard operating procedure) on management of Haze Situation with reference to indoor and outdoor activities and sports (see table below).

 

At the SCRC/JCRC meeting this evening, we concurred on the following measures should the PSI crosses 100 (we are also monitoring the PM2.5 readings):

 

1)      Any (official hall) outdoor sports and activities will be cancelled.  Wherever possible, please schedule these activities indoor.  If not, the activities should not proceed.

2)      For existing (official hall) indoor activities with the use of KE Communal Hall, the air-con will be switched on from 7pm to 11pm from Monday to Friday.  Please discuss with the Residential Life team (i.e., Hidayu, Kellie) should you have any scheduled activities during the weekend.

3)      An additional measure by OHS is that the air-con will be switched on during meal times.

 

Separately, if there is anyone with special medical conditions requiring special arrangements, could you please see me?

 

Keep safe and take care.

 

Regards

 

Kooi Cheng

Safety Advisory to NUS Community - Haze Situation

 

1.    Over the weekend Singapore experienced smoke haze due to hot spots mostly from the central and southern Sumatra, Indonesia. We may still experience slightly hazy conditions in the coming days if the prevailing wind conditions continue to direct smoke haze towards Singapore.

 

2.    Health Advisory – As of 16 Sep 2019, the advisory from NEA is as follows “Given the current air quality forecast, normal activities can largely be continued. However, caution should still be exercised, and persons who are not feeling well, especially the elderly and children, and those with chronic heart or lung conditions, should seek medical attention. The impact of haze is dependent on one’s health status, the PSI level, and the length and intensity of outdoor activity. Reducing outdoor activities and physical exertion can help limit the ill effects from haze exposure.

(ref: https://www.haze.gov.sg/)

 

3.    We recommend that staff and students, who have to work outdoors for prolonged periods (continuous exposure for several hours), doing field work, construction, maintenance, supervision, inspection, outdoor events and sports, can refer to and shall adopt the existing NUS Safety SOP (standard operating procedure) on management of Haze Situation. The SOP can be accessed via:

https://share.nus.edu.sg/corporate/procedures/emergency_mgt/Crisis-Management/Response-Plans-and-Guidelines/NUS%20Haze%20Mitigation%20Measures%20Update%20to%20PSM%20121114.pdf

 

4.    You may also refer to the following channels for updates:

 

a.      NEA website (www.nea.gov.sg)

b.     The Haze microsite (www.haze.gov.sg)

c.      MSS website (www.weather.gov.sg)

d.     Mobile apps (myEnv and Weather@SG)

e.      NEA Facebook (www.facebook.com/NEASingapore)

f.      NEA Twitter (@NESsg)

 

5.    NUS is closely monitoring the situation and will update NUS staff and students accordingly, including any additional measures to be taken.
