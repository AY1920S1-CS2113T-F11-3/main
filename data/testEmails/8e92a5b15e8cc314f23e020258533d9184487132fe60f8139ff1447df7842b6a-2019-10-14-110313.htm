CAREER ACCELERATOR<\/p>\r\n

Building Your Leadership Profile for Career Success<\/h1>\r\n<\/td>\r\n<\/tr>\r\n

\r\n

Hi ZI YUN,<\/p>\r\n

How do leaders step up to influence people, shape culture and create high performing teams?<\/p>\r\n

Join Richard Wong<\/strong>, General Manager at Pratt & Whitney TOS<\/strong>, one of the world\u2019s leading engineering firms in the aerospace sector for this Accelerator workshop. He will share his insights on how you can start building your\r\n leadership capabilities now to make an impact at the workplace.<\/p>\r\n

By attending this experiential workshop, students will learn how to:<\/p>\r\n

    \r\n
  1. Gain Insight:<\/strong> Learn how leadership potential in new hires is assessed<\/li>
  2. Create impactful first impressions:<\/strong> Display leadership potential from day one<\/li>
  3. Understand strategies for success:<\/strong> Cultivate leading and influencing capabilities<\/li>
  4. Manage relationships:<\/strong> Understand the needs and drivers of peers to influence positively<\/li><\/ol>\r\n<\/td>\r\n<\/tr>\r\n
\r\n

Speaker<\/h2>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n
<\/td>\r\n\r\n

Richard Wong<\/strong>
\r\nGeneral Manager
\r\nPratt & Whitney Turbine and Overhaul Systems (TOS)<\/p>\r\n

Richard brings more than 30 years of aerospace experience to his current role as General Manager. He started his career as a workshop engineer in 1987 with the then Singapore Engine Overhaul Centre, which later became Pratt & Whitney\u2019s Singapore-based engine\r\n center, Eagle Services Asia, in 1998.<\/p>\r\n

Richard currently sits in the Management Committee of Association of Aerospace Industries Singapore (AAIS) as Vice President, NTU Industry Advisory Panel (Aerospace Engineering) and RMIT Aerospace Engineering & Aviation Industry Advisory Committee.<\/p>\r\n<\/td>\r\n<\/tr>\r\n<\/tbody>\r\n<\/table>\r\n<\/td>\r\n<\/tr>\r\n
